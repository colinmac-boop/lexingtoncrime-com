Kentucky Violent Crime Rate

FBI Uniform Crime Reporting data for Kentucky, 2019 through 2023 (the most recent year published by the FBI), broken down by offense and compared to the U.S. average.

The headline numbers

In 2023, Kentucky's violent crime rate was 231.8 violent offenses per 100,000 residents, well below the national rate of 385.9. Kentucky is one of the lower-violent-crime states in the country — but the picture differs by offense. The murder rate ran slightly above the U.S. average (6.4 vs. 6.1), while robbery and aggravated assault were materially lower than national norms.

Like the rest of the country, Kentucky saw a sharp rise in violent crime during the 2020–2021 pandemic period, peaking at 273.0 per 100,000 in 2020. By 2023 the rate had returned roughly to its 2019 level. Murder followed the same pattern, rising from 5.2 to 8.5 per 100,000 between 2019 and 2021, then falling to 6.4 by 2023.

Property crime in Kentucky has trended downward across the five-year window — burglary is down 32% from 2019, larceny down 22% — with one clear exception: motor vehicle theft, which jumped 26% in 2023.

About this data

  • Source: Federal Bureau of Investigation — Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program, retrieved from the FBI Crime Data Explorer (cde.ucr.cjis.gov).
  • Coverage: Statewide totals for Kentucky compiled from law enforcement agencies that report to the FBI's National Incident-Based Reporting System (NIBRS). Coverage is not 100% of agencies; the FBI applies estimation for non-reporters.
  • Rates: Offenses per 100,000 residents, calculated by the FBI using U.S. Census population estimates.
  • Most recent year: 2023. The FBI typically releases the prior year's full statistics in September. 2024 numbers will publish in late 2025/early 2026.
  • Comparability caveat: The FBI transitioned reporting from the older Summary Reporting System (SRS) to NIBRS in 2021. Some pre-2021 comparisons in older sources may not be directly comparable.
