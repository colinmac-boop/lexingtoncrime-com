Chevy Chase

Lexington, Kentucky

🚶 Most Walkable 🏠 Historic (1920s-30s) 🏪 Euclid Ave Shops

About Chevy Chase

One of Lexington's most historic and sought-after neighborhoods, often ranked as the city's most walkable. Developed in the 1920s and 30s, Chevy Chase features Craftsman Bungalows, Tudor Revivals, and Colonial Revivals centered around the Chevy Chase Shops at Euclid Avenue and East High Street.

Local landmarks include the Chevy Chase Inn — Lexington's oldest bar, opened in 1933 — along with beloved spots like Bourbon n' Toulouse for Cajun cuisine and Josie's for award-winning breakfast. Easy access to Ashland: The Henry Clay Estate, Woodland Park, and The Arboretum.

It remains one of Lexington's most desirable places to live, combining historic character with walkable convenience.

Safety Tips for Chevy Chase

🚶

Walking & Biking

Chevy Chase is highly walkable. Stay on well-lit streets at night, especially around Euclid Avenue and East High Street.

🚗

Vehicle Safety

Don't leave valuables visible in parked cars. Lock your vehicle, even in driveways. Catalytic converter thefts have been reported in the area.

🏠

Home Security

Older homes may have fewer security features. Consider smart locks, cameras, and motion lights. Join the Chevy Chase Neighborhood Association for community watch updates.

Crime & Safety in Chevy Chase

Is Chevy Chase Lexington safe? This historic neighborhood is generally considered one of the safer areas in Lexington KY, thanks to its active community involvement and walkable design. Chevy Chase Lexington KY crime rates tend to be lower than city averages, particularly for violent crimes.

Chevy Chase neighborhood safety benefits from strong community engagement and good street lighting along major corridors. Property crimes like vehicle break-ins can occur, so residents should take standard precautions with car and home security. The neighborhood's walkability and established community watch efforts contribute to overall safety in this desirable Lexington area.
