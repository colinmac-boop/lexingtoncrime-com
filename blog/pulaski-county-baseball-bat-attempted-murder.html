Ferguson KY Man Charged With Attempted Murder After Baseball Bat Attack on Two People

May 6, 2026 · 2 min read · Source: LEX 18

A 44-year-old Ferguson, Kentucky man is facing two counts of attempted murder after allegedly attacking two people with a baseball bat early Sunday morning, leaving both victims hospitalized with serious injuries.

According to the Pulaski County Sheriff's Office, deputies were dispatched to a residence on Waddle Street at approximately 5:43 a.m. after a 911 call reported that a man — later identified as Jonathan Gross — had struck the caller and his girlfriend with a baseball bat.

The caller told dispatchers he and his girlfriend were able to escape from Gross long enough to make the emergency call. When deputies arrived on scene, they found a woman outside the residence bleeding from a head wound. She told officers that Gross had struck her in the head with the bat and that he had barricaded himself inside a bedroom.

After multiple attempts to get Gross to exit the bedroom voluntarily, deputies entered the room and took him into custody without further incident.

Charges Filed

Gross was booked on the following charges:

  • Two counts of attempted murder
  • Second-degree disorderly conduct
  • Two counts of third-degree terroristic threatening
  • Alcohol intoxication in a public place

Both victims were transported to a local hospital for treatment of their injuries. The sheriff's office has not released further details on their conditions.

About Pulaski County

Pulaski County is located in south-central Kentucky, approximately 80 miles south of Lexington. The county seat is Somerset. While the county is largely rural, incidents of violent crime — particularly domestic and alcohol-related violence — do occur. Residents are encouraged to report suspicious activity to the Pulaski County Sheriff's Office.
