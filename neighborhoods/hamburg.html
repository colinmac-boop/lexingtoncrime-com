Lexington, Kentucky

🏗️ Master-Planned 🛍️ Hamburg Pavilion 🛣️ I-75 / I-64 Access

About Hamburg

Hamburg is Lexington's largest master-planned community, developed on the city's east side beginning in the early 2000s. The area is anchored by the Hamburg Pavilion, a massive retail and entertainment center with over 2 million square feet of shopping, dining, and services — making it one of the largest open-air retail destinations in the state.

The neighborhood offers a wide range of housing options, from starter townhomes and apartments to single-family homes and upscale developments. Its location at the intersection of I-75 and I-64 provides easy access to the rest of the Bluegrass region, while still being just minutes from downtown Lexington. Schools, parks, and medical facilities are all within the Hamburg footprint.

Hamburg continues to expand with new residential phases and commercial development, making it one of Lexington's fastest-growing areas and a popular choice for families and young professionals alike.

The area is served by Fayette County's newer schools, including Frederick Douglass High School, which opened in 2017 as the first new high school in Lexington in decades. Hamburg also features the Regal Hamburg Pavilion cinema, TopGolf, and a growing lineup of national and local restaurant chains along Sir Barton Way. The community's newer construction means most homes come equipped with modern security features, energy-efficient design, and open floor plans preferred by today's buyers.

Safety Tips for Hamburg

🛍️

Shopping Area Awareness

Hamburg Pavilion draws large crowds. Keep bags close, lock purchases in the trunk, and be aware of your surroundings in parking lots — especially during holiday seasons.

🚗

Traffic & Road Safety

Sir Barton Way and Man o' War Boulevard see heavy traffic. Use extra caution at intersections and watch for pedestrians crossing between retail areas.

🏠

Community Vigilance

Get to know your neighbors and report suspicious activity. Many Hamburg subdivisions have HOAs and active community groups that help maintain safety.
