Safest Neighborhoods in Lexington KY (2026 Guide)

Whether you're moving to Lexington, Kentucky or just looking for a safer neighborhood to call home, this guide breaks down the top 10 safest areas in the city based on crime data and community reputation.

Lexington, Kentucky — the Horse Capital of the World — is a city of roughly 320,000 people with a mix of historic charm, college-town energy, and suburban sprawl. Like any mid-sized American city, safety varies significantly from one neighborhood to the next. If you're researching where to live in Lexington KY, crime data should be one factor in your decision.

Below are 10 of the safest neighborhoods in Lexington, based on reported crime density, community feedback, and overall livability. For current crime data in any of these areas, check our interactive Lexington crime map.

1. Andover

Andover consistently ranks among the safest areas in Lexington. This master-planned subdivision on the city's south side features well-maintained homes, manicured common areas, and an active homeowners association. Families are drawn here by the excellent schools in the area, including Stonewall Elementary and Jesse Clark Middle School. The neighborhood's low crime rates and strong community engagement make it a top choice for families with children.

2. Hartland

Hartland is one of Lexington's premier residential communities, located on the south side of the city. Known for its spacious lots, walking trails, and multiple swimming pools, Hartland offers a suburban lifestyle with quick access to shopping and dining along Nicholasville Road. Crime rates here remain consistently low, and the subdivision's layout — with limited entry points and cul-de-sac streets — naturally discourages through traffic and opportunistic crime.

3. Chevy Chase

Chevy Chase is one of Lexington's most beloved neighborhoods, centered around the Chevy Chase Place shopping area on Euclid Avenue. This walkable, tree-lined neighborhood features Craftsman and Colonial-style homes, many dating to the early 1900s. Its proximity to the University of Kentucky brings cultural amenities while maintaining a residential feel. The strong sense of community and high foot traffic help keep crime low relative to its urban location.

4. Palomar Hills

Located in the southern part of Lexington near Man o' War Boulevard, Palomar Hills is a well-established neighborhood known for its family-friendly environment. The area features mature trees, a community pool, and access to highly rated schools. Property crime and violent crime alike remain low, making it a reliable choice for homebuyers looking for safety and value in Lexington.

5. Hamburg

Hamburg is one of Lexington's fastest-growing areas, located on the east side near I-75 and I-64. This master-planned community combines residential neighborhoods with retail, dining, and entertainment at Hamburg Pavilion. The newer construction, well-lit streets, and commercial activity create an area with relatively low crime. Families appreciate the proximity to parks, new schools, and the convenience of nearby shopping.

6. Ashland Park

Ashland Park is a historic gem near Henry Clay's Ashland estate. This National Register historic district was designed by the Olmsted Brothers and features stately homes along tree-lined streets. The neighborhood's affluence, community pride, and established character all contribute to its strong safety record. It's one of the most sought-after addresses in Lexington for a reason.

7. Idle Hour

Idle Hour takes its name from the historic Idle Hour Stock Farm and sits in south-central Lexington. This quiet, established neighborhood features mid-century homes on generous lots with mature landscaping. The area benefits from its proximity to good schools and the Idle Hour Country Club. Crime incidents are infrequent, and the neighborhood's residential nature keeps it off the radar for most types of property crime.

8. Masterson Station

Masterson Station is a large residential area in northwest Lexington, named after the adjacent Masterson Station Park — one of the largest parks in the city with over 600 acres of trails and green space. The neighborhood combines newer suburban development with recreational access that's hard to beat. Crime rates stay low thanks to the area's family-oriented demographics and distance from higher-crime corridors.

9. Beaumont

Beaumont is a planned community in eastern Lexington that has grown rapidly over the past decade. With a mix of single-family homes, townhouses, and a village center with shops and restaurants, Beaumont offers a walkable, modern lifestyle. The neighborhood's design emphasizes connectivity and community gathering, and its relatively new infrastructure means well-lit streets and modern security considerations. It's a solid option for those seeking safety in a newer development.

10. Bell Court

Bell Court is a small, tight-knit neighborhood just south of downtown Lexington. Despite its central location, Bell Court maintains a quiet, residential character with well-kept homes and an active neighborhood association. The combination of community vigilance and walkability to downtown amenities makes it safer than its urban location might suggest. It's an excellent choice for those who want to be close to the action without sacrificing security.
