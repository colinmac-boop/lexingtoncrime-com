Neighborhood Safety Guides

Wondering where to live in Lexington KY? Explore our comprehensive guides to the best neighborhoods Lexington has to offer, complete with safety data and local insights.

🏘️

Chevy Chase

One of Lexington's most historic and sought-after neighborhoods — often ranked the city's most walkable.

  • • Most walkable neighborhood in Lexington
  • • Developed in the 1920s and 30s
  • • Craftsman Bungalows and Tudor Revivals
🏙️

Downtown Lexington

Exciting urban lifestyle with historic districts, modern condos, vibrant nightlife, and walkable access to everything.

  • • Historic Gratz Park district (est. 1781)
  • • Distillery District and North Lime
  • • Urban living with historic charm
🏬

Hamburg

Premier master-planned community in southeastern Lexington surrounding the massive Hamburg Pavilion.

  • • Master-planned community
  • • Hamburg Pavilion — 2M+ sq ft of retail
  • • I-75 and I-64 access
🏛️

Ashland Park

National Register historic district designed by the Olmsted Brothers — the firm behind Central Park.

  • • National Register of Historic Places
  • • Olmsted Brothers landscape design
  • • Henry Clay's Ashland estate
⛳

Andover

Upscale, highly desirable neighborhood in southeast Lexington with large homes and mature tree-lined streets.

  • • Upscale neighborhood
  • • Andover Golf and Country Club
  • • Mature tree-lined streets
🌳

Idle Hour

Charming established mid-century neighborhood near Richmond Road with mature trees and large lots.

  • • Mid-century character
  • • Large mature lots
  • • Winding streets
🔔

Bell Court

Historic neighborhood and Preserve America Community just southeast of Downtown, known for charm and walkability.

  • • Historic district
  • • Preserve America Community
  • • Walkable neighborhood
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Masterson Station

Family-friendly neighborhood with open green spaces, a community park, and convenient access to I-75.

  • • Family-friendly atmosphere
  • • Masterson Station Park
  • • Open green spaces
