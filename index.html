Crime Categories

Understand the types of crime reported in Lexington.

🏠

Theft

Taking property without force or threat — shoplifting, package theft, theft from a vehicle. Differs from burglary (entering a building) and robbery (using force).
🔓

Burglary

Entering a building or structure unlawfully to commit a crime — usually theft. The defining element is the unlawful entry itself, not the presence of force.
💰

Robbery

Taking property directly from a person using force, threat, or intimidation. Differs from theft (no contact) and burglary (no person present).
👊

Assault

A physical attack or credible threat of attack against a person. Aggravated assault involves a weapon or serious injury; simple assault does not.
🔥

Arson

Deliberately setting fire to a building, vehicle, or other property. Includes attempts that don't result in significant damage.
🔫

Shooting

An incident involving discharge of a firearm — whether or not anyone is struck. Includes drive-by shootings and confirmed shots-fired calls.
🎨

Vandalism

Intentional damage or defacement of property without taking it — graffiti, broken windows, slashed tires.
🚔

Arrest

A logged arrest by the responding agency. Often follows another incident type — listed separately because the public report is the arrest itself.
📋

Other

Incidents that don't fit the standard set — drug offenses, weapon violations, traffic incidents, and miscellaneous public-order calls.
